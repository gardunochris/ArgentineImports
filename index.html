    OUR EXPERIENCE

    "if opportunity doesn't knock, Build A Door."

    My work experience started when I was 12 years old, cleaning a small bar in the neighborhood where I lived, there my interest in personal and professional progress grew. Then I went into apartment cleaning until I went out to preach the gospel as a full-time missionary in Buenos Aires, where I met great people, including my personal friend Mark Pitman, who after finishing our two years of service lost contact because of life gets busy.

    In the course of my life, I went from job to job, always trying to improve myself and performing every task that was required of me, until I could get a great position in the port of Villa Gobernador Galvez where the Cargill has operations. There I excelled in the business of unloading trucks for seven years. In 2018 I was laid off along with many of my beloved coworkers due to changes in international export markets.

    Thanks to my experience at Cargill, today I am presented with a great blessing: A year and a half ago, thank God and our mission for Jesus Christ we were able to reconnect with Mark Pitman, who gave me the opportunity to start my business of exporting solar panels and grains, which I know thanks to my performance at Cargill.

    This is how my family business, Campos FV began to grow.
